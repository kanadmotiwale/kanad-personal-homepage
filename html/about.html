About Me

From Electronics Engineering to Software Development

My Journey

My path into software wasn't exactly straight. I started with Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering in Mumbai, but quickly realized I was spending more time coding than working with circuits. That's when I knew software development was where I belonged.

The real learning happened at LTIMindtree, where I got thrown into building enterprise systems that needed to handle thousands of requests without breaking. Those 2+ years taught me that good software isn't just about making it work — it's about making it work when everything else is falling apart.

Co-founding FastFindFirm was a completely different challenge. Going from zero to 5,000 users meant I had to think about scalability from day one. Watching it grow taught me as much about business as it did about technology.

Now at Northeastern, I'm diving deeper into distributed systems, machine learning, and advanced algorithms — finally understanding the "why" behind the patterns I've been using in production.
